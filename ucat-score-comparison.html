üìä Is This Tool Reliable?

Quadratic Model Explanation
Because the relationship between the 2024 and 2025 scores wasn‚Äôt perfectly linear ‚Äî with the linear model showing a slight bias at the lower end ‚Äî a quadratic model was used. A quadratic model takes the form:

\[ \text{2025 Score} = a \cdot (\text{2024 Score})^2 + b \cdot (\text{2024 Score}) + c \]

Using the paired data from the percentile thresholds, we fitted the following quadratic equation:

\[ \text{2025 Score} \approx -0.000056 \cdot (\text{2024 Score})^2 + 1.536980 \cdot (\text{2024 Score}) - 148.88 \]

This equation provides a closer approximation across the spectrum of scores since it allows for curvature in the relationship.

Rearranged Equation
For cases where you might want to input a 2025 score and predict the corresponding 2024 score, we solved the quadratic equation for the 2024 score. The rearranged equation is:

\[ \text{2024 Score} \approx 13723.04 - 13625.83 \cdot \sqrt{1 - 0.00009618 \cdot (\text{2025 Score})} \]

(We use the minus version of the +- solution because the resulting values fall into the expected range for 2024 scores.)
